Suggested Participants (4-8 people total)

  • Pastoral/religious leadership (imam, rabbi, pastor)
  • Charitable ministry leaders
  • Administrative/logistical coordinators
  • One board/governance representative

Meeting Structure (60-90 minutes recommended)

1. Opening and Grounding (10-15 minutes)

Facilitator: Begin with shared scripture reading (e.g., Isaiah 40:3-5 or Luke 3:4-6).

Each participant: Briefly introduce themselves and their community's charitable work.

Shared affirmation:
"We gather to explore whether coordinated preparation might bear greater fruit for the vulnerable, while fully protecting our distinct identities and primary obligations."

2. Sharing Current Realities (20 minutes)

Guiding questions (each community responds briefly):

  • What are your community's main charitable activities?
  • What "second coat" resources do you have (facilities, volunteers, purchasing needs)?
  • What challenges do you face (gaps during observances, administrative burden, costs)?
  • How do religious calendars affect service continuity?
Principle: Listen without judgment; identify complementarity, not competition.

  • Phase 1 focus (efficiency only‚Äîno financial redirection)
  • Voluntariness and consequence-free exit
  • Fruit as the test

Key clarification questions:

  • Does this distinction between preparation and possession resonate?
  • Does the Second Coat Principle adequately protect your primary obligations?
  • Do you see potential complementarity in our calendars/resources?

4. Exploring a Possible Phase 1 Pilot (20 minutes)

Brainstorm together:

  • What one concrete opportunity might we explore (joint purchasing, scheduling, facilities)?
  • What fruit metrics would matter to both communities?
  • What concerns or boundaries need addressing upfront?

Important: No decisions today‚Äîonly exploration.
